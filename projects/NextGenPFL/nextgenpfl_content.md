# Project Summary

Analogue and quantum simulators have unique potential to reshape the future of science and technology. By relying on physical systems that follow dynamics isomorphic to a given target model, they allow to simulate scenarios of high complexity in faster and energy-efficient manners when compared against numerical simulations. Paraxial fluids of light(PFL) are one of such platforms: following dynamics close to the Schrödinger equation, they offer light-speed quantum simulations for 2D quantum fluids. Yet, while this potential has been validated, there are still missing links to materialize the potential of these platforms for practical applications.

Merging the teams from Kastler-Brossel Laboratory at University of Sorbonne and INESCTEC, this project is an effort to achieve a common vision-to harness the true potential of PFLs as quantum simulators-by addressing their limitations, enhancing their accessibility to the community, and establishing a roadmap towards their application.

#### Objective 2 - Establish a road map for Paraxial Fluids of Light as Analogue and Quantum Simulations Platforms

This objective aims to define a roadmap for using paraxial fluids of light to simulate quantum matter and explore new technological applications. At the same time, it will map which dynamics can be explored using digital-feedback-loop-like implementations.

This project has the following partner:

#### [Quantul FLuids of Light from the Kastler-Brossel Laboratory at University of Sorbonne](https://www.quentinglorieux.fr)
The Quantum Fluids of Light Lab team at the Kastler-Brossel Laboratory (LKB) in France is currently at the forefront of paraxial fluids of light research, with extensive expertise from both theoretical and experimental perspectives. With numerous works published in major journals reporting significant advances in the field, the LKB team has a robust foundation capable of driving the necessary innovations for the next generation of paraxial fluids of light.
